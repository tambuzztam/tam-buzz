## The Core Distinction

Philosophical counseling addresses human beings as **growing** and in their growth as **growing beings**. This is fundamentally different from therapeutic approaches that address how you're **faring**.

## What It Means to Address Someone as Faring

Traditional therapy focuses on:

- How are you doing?
- How are you feeling?
- Are you feeling okay?
- What are your symptoms?
- Are you well or are you suffering?
- Your present state and wellbeing

**Therapy is concerned with your current condition**—whether you're functioning well, managing symptoms, or achieving stability.

## What It Means to Address Someone as Growing

Philosophical counseling focuses on:

- How are you growing?
- How have you grown?
- Where have you come from?
- Where are you going?
- What are you becoming?
- What must you become?
- What is the core of who you are?

**Philosophical counseling is concerned with your trajectory**—the arc of your development and the expansion of who you're becoming.

## The Mathematics of Growth

Growing being + more growing being + stronger growing being = **Enhancement of what is already there**

This is not about:

- Adding something foreign to yourself
- Fixing what's broken
- Returning to a previous state
- Eliminating unwanted aspects

Instead, it's about **strengthening and developing the elements of growth that are already present**.

## Growth as Natural Response

Human beings naturally respond to what's important by growing. This is:

- Your **natural response** to encountering what matters
- The **only adequate response** to what's truly significant
- **Already happening** in your life, whether you notice it or not

## Why This Matters

This distinction shapes everything:

- **What questions we ask**: Not "What's wrong?" but "What's growing?"
- **How we listen**: For signs of development, not symptoms of dysfunction
- **What we honor**: Your trajectory toward becoming, not just your current state
- **How we measure success**: By growth and strengthening, not by symptom reduction

## Both Approaches Have Value

This isn't about dismissing therapy—it has its place and importance. The distinction is about recognizing that:

- Different approaches serve different needs
- Growing beings may not need therapeutic intervention
- Growth work has its own methodology and focus
- You can be "faring well" but still have enormous capacity for growth

## The Seeds Are Already There

Notice how growth is the enhancement or strengthening of something **already present**. Even from the beginning, the roots or elements of your growth are there. The work is about:

- Recognizing what's already growing
- Providing conditions for flourishing
- Strengthening natural development
- Honoring the trajectory you're already on
